1
Initial Patient Inquiry
Scheduling & pre-screening
Active

Patient contacts practice requesting a travel medicine consultation. Confirm membership status and collect initial travel details before scheduling.

Verify patient membership status in EHR
Collect travel destination(s) and departure date
Note trip duration and type (leisure / business / expedition)
Schedule appointment ≥ 4–6 weeks before departure if possible
Send intake form link to patient (pre-travel health questionnaire)
Document inquiry in travel medicine tracker spreadsheet

2
Pre-Visit Health Assessment
Review intake form & medical history
Active

Before the appointment, the physician reviews the patient's submitted intake form and existing medical records to prepare a personalized consultation plan.

Review completed pre-travel health questionnaire
Check current medications for potential travel drug interactions
Note chronic conditions requiring special travel considerations
Review past vaccination records (identify gaps)
Research destination-specific health risks (CDC Travelers' Health)
Identify required vs. recommended vaccines for destination
Check if Yellow Fever certificate required (e.g., Sub-Saharan Africa, Amazon)
3
Vaccine & Immunization Planning
Required & recommended vaccines by destination
In Progress

Develop a personalized immunization plan based on destination, duration, and patient immune history. Administer vaccines in-office or coordinate referral for Yellow Fever.

Vaccine Indication Schedule Status
Hepatitis A All international travel 2-dose series Review
Typhoid South Asia, Africa, Latin America Oral or IM, 2 yr booster Order
Yellow Fever Required: Sub-Saharan Africa, Amazon Single dose (10-yr validity) Coordinate
Malaria Prophylaxis Malaria-endemic regions See Step 4 medications Rx
Rabies (Pre-Exposure) Remote areas, outdoor activities 3-dose series over 21 days Assess
Japanese Encephalitis Rural Asia, extended stays 2-dose (Ixiaro) Assess

Document all vaccines administered with lot #, site, and date
Issue International Certificate of Vaccination (Yellow Card) if applicable
Update EHR immunization record
4

Malaria prophylaxis, altitude sickness, traveler's diarrhea

Prescribe travel-specific medications based on destination risks, trip type, and patient's medical history. Provide patient education on each medication.

5
Consultation & Counseling
In-office or telemedicine visit
Pending

Comprehensive travel health consultation covering safety, food/water precautions, insect protection, environmental risks, and emergency action plan.

Food & water safety counseling (boil, bottled, avoid raw)
Mosquito/insect protection (DEET 30%+, permethrin, bed nets)
Sun exposure & heat illness prevention
Altitude acclimatization guidance (if applicable)
Sexual health & STI prevention (if relevant destination)
Emergency travel insurance & evacuation coverage discussed
Nearest US Embassy / emergency contacts provided
Provider's emergency contact shared with patient
Patient Travel Health Summary letter issued (for customs/border)
Complete visit note in EHR
6
Documentation & Follow-Up
EHR, tracker, post-return check-in
Pending

Finalize and sign EHR visit note
Update shared Travel Medicine Tracker (patient, destination, vaccines, RX)

Send patient post-trip resources (symptom awareness guide)
Flag any vaccine series requiring booster (calendar reminder)
